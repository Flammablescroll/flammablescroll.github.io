Hexadecimals are pretty interesting.

A brief explanation of hexadecimals

Hexadecimals are a base 16 number system, the number system we normally use is base 10. Because hexadecimals are a base 16 number system this means it goes from 0-9, then A-F, and after F they loop back to 0 and add a digit to the number. As opposed to 0-9 which we are used too.
One major use for hexadecimals is storing colors. There are three main colors that hexadecimals store, red, green, and blue. By combining different percentages of these three colors we can change the hue and make new colors. We store each of these colors as 2 hexadecimal digits, all in one string, red would be #FF0000.
The FF is the red, and FF is the highest a 2 digit hexadecimal can go. The four 0s are the green and blue, both of them are set to the lowest hexadecimal value.

Why should you care about HTML Whitespace?

The importance of Whitespace in HTML

In HTML and almost any other programming language, whitespace is crucial to having a readable program and website. Having bad whitespace can make a program almost unreadable.
This may be okay if you're the only one working on it, but if you are a part of a team, depending on how bad your whitespacing is, your coworkers can range from being annoyed to almost having an aneurysm trying to read your code.
To put it another way, imagine trying to read a book or an article without any punctuation at all, it's doable, but it will not be a fun time.

Fonts can make a bold statement.

The usefulness of fonts

Fonts are important to what vibe you want to give your website. There are countless different types of fonts and ways to customize your text, so it may seem a bit overwhelming. But fear not, for I am here.
There are four main things you want to focus on when making your font, color, size, weight, and family.

Color: The color of a font is usually black, but if you want to spice it up a little try using the opposite color of your websites background.

Size: The size is usually dictated by the subsection it is on. For example the h1 header of your website will usually be biggest text on that page.

Weight: Your font weight is how thick or thin your text will be, keep in mind this will change the size of your text, but it wont be that noticeable of a difference.

Font Family: A font family is the type of font, such as Times-new-roman, I'm using Futura for this blog.

The font you choose is really just personal preference. One thing to consider is to take into account what type of website you are making when choosing the style of your font.
